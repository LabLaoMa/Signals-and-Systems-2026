连续时间傅里叶变换

Fourier transform (分析公式)

\[ X(j \omega)=\mathcal{F}\{x\}(j \omega)=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} x(t) e^{-j \omega t} d t \]

\(X(j \omega)\) called 频谱 of \(x(t)\)

逆傅里叶变换 (综合公式)

\[ x(t)=\mathcal{F}^{-1}\{X\}(t)=\frac{1}{2 \pi} \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} X(j \omega) e^{j \omega t} d \omega \]

Superposition of complex exponentials at continuum of frequencies; frequency \(\omega\) has density \(\frac{1}{2 \pi} X(j \omega)\)

连续时间傅里叶变换

Two equivalent representations of same signal

  • 时域 vs. 频域: \(x(t) \stackrel{\mathcal{F}}{\longleftrightarrow} X(j \omega)\)

Also widely used elsewhere. In probability theory,

  • characteristic function of random variable \(X\) with density \(p(x)\)

\[ \varphi_{X}(t)=\mathbb{E}\left[e^{j t X}\right]=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} p(x) e^{j x t} d x \]

In quantum mechanics

  • position representation: wave function \(\psi(x)\)
  • \(|\psi(x)|^{2}\) probability density of finding particle at position \(x\)
  • momentum representation: \(\Psi(p)\)

\[ \Psi(p)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2 \pi \hbar}} \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \psi(x) e^{-j p x / \hbar} d x \]

  • \(|\Psi(p)|^{2}\) probability density of finding particle with momentum \(p\)

\(L_1\) 信号的傅里叶变换

Recall from calculus, for real-valued \(x\), improper integral

\[ \int_{\mathbb{R}} x(t) d t \triangleq \lim _{T_{1}, T_{2} \rightarrow \infty} \int_{-T_{1}}^{T_{2}} x(t) d t \]

is well-defined if \(x \in L_{1}(\mathbb{R})\), i.e. \(\|x\|_{1}=\int_{\mathbb{R}}|x(t)| d t<\infty\) Same is true for complex-valued \(x=u+j v\), since \(|u|,|v| \leq|x|\) and

\[ \int_{\mathbb{R}} x(t) d t \triangleq \int_{\mathbb{R}} u(t) d t+j \int_{\mathbb{R}} v(t) d t \]

For signal \(x \in L_{1}(\mathbb{R})\)

  • \(x(t) e^{-j \omega t} \in L_{1}(\mathbb{R})\), since \(\left|x(t) e^{-j \omega t}\right|=|x(t)|\)
  • Fourier transform \(X(j \omega)=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} x(t) e^{-j \omega t} d t\) is well-defined improper integral for each \(\omega\)

\(L_1\) 信号的傅里叶变换

定理. If \(x \in L_{1}\), then \(X\) is bounded, in fact \(\|X\|_{\infty} \leq\|x\|_{1}\) 证明. Consequence of following lemma. 引理. For complex-valued function \(f\) of real variable \(t\),

\[ \left|\int f(t) d t\right| \leq \int|f(t)| d t \]

证明. If \(\int|f(t)| d t=\infty\), trivial. Assume \(\int|f(t)| d t<\infty\). Let phase of \(\int f(t) d t\) be \(\phi\), i.e. \(\int f(t) d t=\left|\int f(t) d t\right| e^{j \phi}\). Then

\[ \left|\int f(t) d t\right|=e^{-j \phi} \int f(t) d t=\int e^{-j \phi} f(t) d t \]

Taking real part

\[ \left|\int f(t) d t\right|=\operatorname{Re} \int e^{-j \phi} f(t) d t=\int \operatorname{Re}\left[e^{-j \phi} f(t)\right] d t \leq \int\left|e^{-j \phi} f(t)\right| d t \]

\(L_1\) 信号的傅里叶变换

定理. If \(x \in L_{1}\), then \(X\) is uniformly continuous 证明. For any \(T>0\),

\[ \begin{aligned} \left|X\left(j \omega_{1}\right)-X\left(j \omega_{2}\right)\right| & \leq \int_{\mathbb{R}}\left|x(t) e^{-j \omega_{1} t}-x(t) e^{-j \omega_{2} t}\right| d t \\ & =\int_{\mathbb{R}}|x(t)| \cdot\left|2 \sin \frac{\Delta \omega t}{2}\right| d t \quad\left(\Delta \omega=\omega_{1}-\omega_{2}\right) \\ & \leq 2 \int_{|t|>T}|x(t)| d t+|\Delta \omega| T \int_{|t| \leq T}|x(t)| d t \\ & \leq 2 \int_{|t|>T}|x(t)| d t+|\Delta \omega| T| | x \|_{1} \triangleq I_{1}+I_{2} \end{aligned} \]

Given \(\epsilon>0\), since \(x \in L_{1}(\mathbb{R})\), exists \(T>0\) s.t. \(I_{1}<\epsilon / 2\). Fix such \(T\). For \(|\Delta \omega| \leq \epsilon /\left(2 T\|x\|_{1}\right), I_{2}<\epsilon / 2\). Thus \(\left|X\left(j \omega_{1}\right)-X\left(j \omega_{2}\right)\right|<\epsilon\).

例: Right-sided Decaying Exponential

\(x(t)=e^{-a t} u(t), \quad a>0\)

\[ \begin{aligned} X(j \omega) & =\int_{0}^{\infty} e^{-(a+j \omega) t} d t \quad 0 \quad 1 / a \\ & =-\left.\frac{1}{a+j \omega} e^{-(a+j \omega) t}\right|_{0} ^{\infty}=\frac{1}{a+j \omega} \end{aligned} \]

NB. Above formula for \(X\) also works for complex \(a\) with \(\operatorname{Re} a>0\).

For \(a>0\),

\[ \begin{aligned} |X(j \omega)| & =\frac{1}{\sqrt{a^{2}+\omega^{2}}} \\ \arg X(j \omega) & =-\arctan \frac{\omega}{a} \end{aligned} \]

例: Two-sided Decaying Exponential

\[ x(t)=e^{-a|t|}, \quad a>0 \]

\[ \begin{aligned} X(j \omega) & =\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} e^{-a|t|} e^{-j \omega t} d t \\ & =\int_{-\infty}^{0} e^{(a-j \omega) t} d t+\int_{0}^{\infty} e^{-(a+j \omega) t} d t \\ & =\frac{1}{a-j \omega}+\frac{1}{a+j \omega} \\ & =\frac{2 a}{a^{2}+\omega^{2}} \end{aligned} \]

NB. Above formula for \(X\) also works for complex \(a\) with \(\operatorname{Re} a>0\).

例: 高斯函数

For \(a>0\),

\[ x(t)=e^{-a t^{2}} \stackrel{\mathcal{F}}{\longleftrightarrow} X(j \omega)=\sqrt{\frac{\pi}{a}} e^{-\frac{\omega^{2}}{4 a}} \]

\[ \begin{aligned} & -a=1 / 2 \\ & -a=1 \\ & -a=2 \end{aligned} \]

In particular, \(x(t)=e^{-\frac{1}{2} t^{2}} \stackrel{\mathcal{F}}{\longleftrightarrow} X(j \omega)=\sqrt{2 \pi} e^{-\frac{1}{2} \omega^{2}}=\sqrt{2 \pi} x(\omega)\), i.e. \(\mathcal{F}\{x\}=\sqrt{2 \pi} x\)

例: 高斯函数

For \(a>0\),

\[ x(t)=e^{-a t^{2}} \stackrel{\mathcal{F}}{\longleftrightarrow} X(j \omega)=\sqrt{\frac{\pi}{a}} e^{-\frac{\omega^{2}}{4 a}} \]

证明.

\[ \begin{aligned} & \frac{d}{d \omega} X(j \omega)=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} e^{-a t^{2}}(-j t) e^{-j \omega t} d t=\frac{j}{2 a} \int_{-\infty}^{\infty}\left(\frac{d}{d t} e^{-a t^{2}}\right) e^{-j \omega t} d t \\ &=-\frac{j}{2 a} \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} e^{-a t^{2}}\left(\frac{d}{d t} e^{-j \omega t}\right) d t \quad \text { (integration by parts) } \\ &=-\frac{\omega}{2 a} \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} e^{-a t^{2}} e^{-j \omega t} d t=-\frac{\omega}{2 a} X(j \omega) \\ & \frac{d}{d \omega}\left(X(j \omega) e^{\frac{\omega^{2}}{4 a}}\right)=0 \Longrightarrow X(j \omega)=X(j 0) e^{-\frac{\omega^{2}}{4 a}}=\sqrt{\frac{\pi}{a}} e^{-\frac{\omega^{2}}{4 a}} \end{aligned} \]

Fourier Inversion for \(L_{1}\) Signals

Given Fourier transform,

\[ X(j \omega)=\mathcal{F}\{x\}(j \omega)=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} x(t) e^{-j \omega t} d t \]

Is inverse Fourier transform well-defined? Is it equal to \(x\) ?

\[ x(t) \stackrel{?}{=} \mathcal{F}^{-1}\{X\}(t)=\frac{1}{2 \pi} \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} X(j \omega) e^{j \omega t} d \omega \]

定理. If \(x \in L_{1}(\mathbb{R})\) is continuous and \(X=\mathcal{F}\{x\} \in L_{1}(\mathbb{R})\), then \(x=\mathcal{F}^{-1}\{X\}\).

  • e.g. Two-sided decaying exponential, 高斯函数

But, for \(x(t) \in L_{1}(\mathbb{R}), X(j \omega)\) is not necessarily in \(L_{1}(\mathbb{R})\)

  • e.g. one-sided decaying exponential, rectangular pulse
  • if \(X \in L_{1}(\mathbb{R}), x\) must be continuous

Fourier Inversion for \(L_{1}\) Signals

Inverse FT typically interpreted as principal value, i.e.

\[ \frac{1}{2 \pi} \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} X(j \omega) e^{j \omega t} d \omega=\lim _{W \rightarrow \infty} \frac{1}{2 \pi} \int_{-W}^{W} X(j \omega) e^{j \omega t} d \omega \]

may converge without being absolutely convergent 定理. If \(x \in L_{1}(\mathbb{R})\) satisfies Dirichlet conditions on all finite intervals, then

\[ \lim _{W \rightarrow \infty} \frac{1}{2 \pi} \int_{-W}^{W} X(j \omega) e^{j \omega t} d \omega=\frac{x\left(t_{+}\right)+x\left(t_{-}\right)}{2} \text { pointwise } \]

NB. Gibbs phenomenon at discontinuity Often also need to interpret FT as principal value

\[ \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} x(t) e^{-j \omega t} d t=\lim _{T \rightarrow \infty} \int_{-T}^{T} x(t) e^{-j \omega t} d t \]

例: 矩形脉冲

\[ \begin{aligned} x(t) & =u(t+T)-u(t-T) \\ X(j \omega) & =\int_{-T}^{T} e^{-j \omega t} d t=\frac{2 \sin (\omega T)}{\omega} \end{aligned} \]

Inverse FT

\[ \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \frac{\sin (\omega T)}{\pi \omega} d \omega=x(0)=1 \]

As \(T \rightarrow \infty\),

  • 频域

\[ \lim _{T \rightarrow \infty} \frac{\sin (\omega T)}{\pi \omega}=\delta(\omega) \]

  • 时域: \(x(t) \rightarrow 1\), DC ✓

\[ \operatorname{sinc}(\theta) \triangleq \frac{\sin (\pi \theta)}{\pi \theta} \]

例: 理想低通滤波器

频率响应

\[ H(j \omega)=u\left(\omega+\omega_{c}\right)-u\left(\omega-\omega_{c}\right) \]

冲激响应

\[ h(t)=\frac{1}{2 \pi} \int_{-\omega_{c}}^{\omega_{c}} e^{j \omega t} d \omega=\frac{\sin \left(\omega_{c} t\right)}{\pi t} \]

NB. \(h(t) \notin L_{1}(\mathbb{R})\) but \(H(j \omega) \in L_{1}(\mathbb{R})\)

As \(\omega_{c} \rightarrow \infty\),

  • 时域
  • \(h(t) \rightarrow \delta(t)\), becomes identity system
  • 频域
  • \(H(j \omega) \rightarrow 1\), passes all frequencies ✓

更一般函数的傅里叶变换

If \(x_{n} \rightarrow x\), define Fourier transform of \(x\) by

\[ X(j \omega)=\mathcal{F}\{x\} \triangleq \lim _{n} \mathcal{F}\left\{x_{n}\right\} \]

i.e.

\[ X(j \omega)=\int_{\mathbb{R}} x(t) e^{-j \omega t} d t \triangleq \lim _{n} \int_{\mathbb{R}} x_{n}(t) e^{-j \omega t} d t \]

If \(x \in L_{1}(\mathbb{R})\), above definition is consistent with old one In general, convergence interpreted in distributional sense, i.e. for nice test function \(\phi\)

\[ \int_{\mathbb{R}} X(j \omega) \phi(\omega) d \omega \triangleq \lim _{n} \int_{\mathbb{R}}\left(\int_{\mathbb{R}} x_{n}(t) e^{-j \omega t} d t\right) \phi(\omega) d \omega \]

Interchanging order of integration leads to alternative definition

\[ \begin{equation*} \int_{\mathbb{R}} X(j \omega) \phi(\omega) d \omega=\lim _{n} \int_{\mathbb{R}} x_{n}(t)\left(\int_{\mathbb{R}} \phi(\omega) e^{-j \omega t} d \omega\right) d t=\int_{\mathbb{R}} x(t) \Phi(j t) d t \tag{18/30} \end{equation*} \]

Schwarz Space

Space of test functions is so-called Schwarz space on \(\mathbb{R}\), denoted \(\mathcal{S}=\mathcal{S}(\mathbb{R})\)

Function \(\phi \in \mathcal{S}\) if it is

  • infinitely differentiable: \(\phi^{(k)}\) exists for all \(k \in \mathbb{N}\)
  • rapidly decreasing:

\[ \|\phi\|_{\ell, k} \triangleq \sup _{t \in \mathbb{R}}\left|t^{\ell} \phi^{(k)}(t)\right|<\infty, \quad \forall \ell, k \in \mathbb{N} \]

例. 高斯函数 \(g(t)=e^{-a t^{2}} \in \mathcal{S}\) Note \(\phi \in \mathcal{S} \Longrightarrow \phi \in L_{1}\), Fourier transform \(\Phi\) well-defined. 定理. If \(\phi \in \mathcal{S}\), then \(\Phi \in \mathcal{S}\) 例. 高斯函数 \(G(j \omega)=\sqrt{\frac{\pi}{a}} e^{-\frac{\omega^{2}}{4 a}} \in \mathcal{S}\)

例: 单位冲激 \(\delta\)

Method 1. Recall for ideal lowpass filter,

\[ h_{W}(t)=\frac{\sin (W t)}{\pi t} \stackrel{\mathcal{F}}{\longleftrightarrow} H_{W}(j \omega)=u(\omega+W)-u(\omega-W) \]

Since \(h_{W} \rightarrow \delta\) as \(W \rightarrow \infty\)

\[ \mathcal{F}\{\delta\}=\lim _{W \rightarrow \infty} H_{W}(j \omega)=1 \]

Can also use 高斯函数 instead of sinc. Recall

\[ g_{a}(t)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{\pi a}} e^{-\frac{t^{2}}{a}} \longleftrightarrow G_{a}(j \omega)=e^{-\frac{a \omega^{2}}{4}} \]

Since \(g_{a} \rightarrow \delta\) as \(a \rightarrow 0\)

\[ \mathcal{F}\{\delta\}=\lim _{a \rightarrow 0} G_{a}(j \omega)=1 \]

例: 单位冲激 \(\delta\)

Method 1. In fact, for any \(x_{n} \rightarrow \delta\),

\[ \mathcal{F}(\delta)=\lim _{n} \int x_{n}(t) e^{-j \omega t} d t=\int_{\mathbb{R}} \delta(t) e^{-j \omega t} d t=e^{-j \omega \cdot 0}=1 \]

Method 2. Let \(X=\mathcal{F}\{\delta\}\).

\[ \int_{\mathbb{R}} X(j \omega) \phi(\omega) d \omega=\int_{\mathbb{R}} \delta(t) \Phi(j t) d t=\Phi(0)=\int_{\mathbb{R}} 1 \cdot \phi(\omega) d \omega \]

Thus \(X(j \omega)=1\). \(\delta\) has “white” 频谱, equal amount of all frequencies! 逆傅里叶变换

\[ \delta(t)=\frac{1}{2 \pi} \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} e^{j \omega t} d \omega=\lim _{W \rightarrow \infty} \frac{1}{2 \pi} \int_{-W}^{W} e^{j \omega t} d \omega=\lim _{W \rightarrow \infty} \frac{\sin (W t)}{\pi t} \]

例: DC Signal 1

Method 1. Recall for rectangular pulse,

\[ x_{T}(t)=u(t+T)-u(t-T) \stackrel{\mathcal{F}}{\longleftrightarrow} X_{T}(j \omega)=\frac{2 \sin (\omega T)}{\omega} \]

Since \(x_{T} \rightarrow 1\) as \(T \rightarrow \infty\)

\[ \mathcal{F}\{1\}=\lim _{T \rightarrow \infty} X_{T}(j \omega)=2 \pi \lim _{T \rightarrow \infty} \frac{\sin (\omega T)}{\pi \omega}=2 \pi \delta(\omega) \]

NB. Same as direct calculation using FT formula Can also use 高斯函数 instead of rectangular pulse. Recall

\[ \tilde{g}_{a}(t)=e^{-a t^{2}} \longleftrightarrow \tilde{G}_{a}(j \omega)=\sqrt{\frac{\pi}{a}} e^{-\frac{\omega^{2}}{4 a}} \]

Since \(\tilde{g}_{a} \rightarrow 1\) as \(a \rightarrow 0\) and \(\left\{\tilde{G}_{a}\right\}\) is family of good kernels

\[ \mathcal{F}\{1\}=\lim _{a \rightarrow 0} \tilde{G}_{a}(j \omega)=\delta(\omega) \]

例: DC Signal 1

Method 2. Let \(X=\mathcal{F}\{1\}\).

\[ \int_{\mathbb{R}} X(j \omega) \phi(\omega) d \omega=\int_{\mathbb{R}} 1 \cdot \Phi(j t) d t=2 \pi \phi(0)=\int_{\mathbb{R}} 2 \pi \delta(\omega) \phi(\omega) d \omega \]

Thus \(X(j \omega)=2 \pi \delta(\omega)\). Formally

\[ 2 \pi \delta(\omega)=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} e^{-j \omega t} d t \]

Spectrum of DC signal is impulse at zero frequency!

逆傅里叶变换

\[ 1=\frac{1}{2 \pi} \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} 2 \pi \delta(\omega) e^{j \omega t} d \omega \]

例: 复指数信号

Let \(X=\mathcal{F}\{x\}\) for \(x(t)=e^{j \omega_{0} t}\).

\[ \begin{aligned} \int_{\mathbb{R}} X(j \omega) \phi(\omega) d \omega & =\int_{\mathbb{R}} e^{j \omega_{0} t} \Phi(j t) d t=2 \pi \phi\left(\omega_{0}\right) \\ & =\int_{\mathbb{R}} 2 \pi \delta\left(\omega-\omega_{0}\right) \phi(\omega) d \omega \end{aligned} \]

Thus \(X(j \omega)=2 \pi \delta\left(\omega-\omega_{0}\right)\). Formally,

\[ 2 \pi \delta\left(\omega-\omega_{0}\right)=\int_{\mathbb{R}} e^{j\left(\omega_{0}-\omega\right) t} d t \]

Spectrum of \(e^{j \omega_{0} t}\) is impulse at \(\omega_{0}\) ! 逆傅里叶变换

\[ e^{j \omega_{0} t}=\frac{1}{2 \pi} \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} 2 \pi \delta\left(\omega-\omega_{0}\right) e^{j \omega t} d \omega \]

例: 复指数信号

\[ 2 \pi \delta\left(\omega_{1}-\omega_{2}\right)=\int_{\mathbb{R}} e^{j\left(\omega_{1}-\omega_{2}\right) t} d t=\int_{\mathbb{R}} e^{j \omega_{1} t} e^{\overline{j \omega_{2} t}} d t \]

Can be interpreted as orthogonality of \(e^{j \omega t}\)

  • \(e^{j \omega_{1} t}\) and \(e^{j \omega_{2} t}\) are orthogonal if \(\omega_{1} \neq \omega_{2}\)

CT Fourier transform can be considered as “orthogonal” expansion into continuum of “basis” functions

\[ X(j \omega)=\left\langle x, e^{j \omega t}\right\rangle=\int_{\mathbb{R}} x(t) \overline{e^{j \omega t}} d t \]

and

\[ x(t)=\frac{1}{2 \pi} \int_{\mathbb{R}}\left\langle x, e^{j \omega t}\right\rangle e^{j \omega t} d \omega \]

周期信号的傅里叶变换

Periodic signal with fundamental frequency \(\omega_{0}\) has Fourier series

\[ x(t)=\sum_{k=-\infty}^{\infty} \hat{x}[k] e^{j k \omega_{0} t} \]

Fourier transform

\[ \begin{aligned} X(j \omega) & =\int_{\mathbb{R}} x(t) e^{-j \omega t} d t=\int_{\mathbb{R}} \sum_{k=-\infty}^{\infty} \hat{x}[k] e^{j k \omega_{0} t} e^{-j \omega t} d t \\ & =\sum_{k=-\infty}^{\infty} \hat{x}[k] \int_{\mathbb{R}} e^{j k \omega_{0} t} e^{-j \omega t} d t=\sum_{k=-\infty}^{\infty} 2 \pi \hat{x}[k] \delta\left(\omega-k \omega_{0}\right) \end{aligned} \]

Spectrum of periodic signal consists of impulses at harmonically related frequencies! Areas of impulses are \(2 \pi\) times Fourier series coefficients

例: Sine and Cosine

\[ \begin{aligned} x_{c}(t) & =\cos \left(\omega_{0} t\right)=\frac{1}{2} e^{j \omega_{0} t}+\frac{1}{2} e^{-j \omega_{0} t} \\ X_{c}(j \omega) & =\pi \delta\left(\omega-\omega_{0}\right)+\pi \delta\left(\omega+\omega_{0}\right) \end{aligned} \]

\[ \begin{aligned} x_{s}(t) & =\sin \left(\omega_{0} t\right)=\frac{1}{2 j} e^{j \omega_{0} t}-\frac{1}{2 j} e^{-j \omega_{0} t} \\ X_{s}(j \omega) & =\frac{\pi}{j} \delta\left(\omega-\omega_{0}\right)-\frac{\pi}{j} \delta\left(\omega+\omega_{0}\right) \end{aligned} \]

例: 周期方波

\[ \hat{x}[k]=\frac{\sin \left(k \omega_{0} T\right)}{k \pi} \]

\[ X(j \omega)=\sum_{k=-\infty}^{\infty} \frac{2 \sin \left(k \omega_{0} T\right)}{k} \delta\left(\omega-k \omega_{0}\right) \]

例: 周期冲激列

\[ x(t)=\sum_{k=-\infty}^{\infty} \delta(t-k T) \stackrel{\mathcal{F}}{\longleftrightarrow} X(j \omega)=\sum_{k=-\infty}^{\infty} \frac{2 \pi}{T} \delta\left(\omega-\frac{2 k \pi}{T}\right) \]
